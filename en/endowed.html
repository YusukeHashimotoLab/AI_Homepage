Endowed Research Division: Nanomaterial Process Data Science

We aim to create an interdisciplinary academic field called "Materials Process Data Science"
by integrating data science with materials process engineering for innovative research.

Research Purpose

This research aims to create an interdisciplinary academic field called "Materials Process Data Science" by integrating data science with materials process engineering.

Specifically, we will:

  • Comprehensively construct a materials-process integrated database for nanoparticle synthesis
  • Extract process characteristic factors that determine material structure and function from the constructed database
  • Establish materials process informatics as a guideline for creating high-performance nanomaterials

This will accelerate next-generation nanomaterial development and innovate materials process engineering.

Latest News

July 31, 2025

Paper Published in APL Machine Learning

A research team led by Specially-Appointed Associate Professor Hashimoto published a paper on creating "materials maps" using AI in the international journal APL Machine Learning.

The study developed materials maps integrating experimental and theoretical calculation data using message-passing graph neural networks. This enables visual representation of material structural similarities and rapid extraction of unknown high-performance materials.

December 11, 2024

Presentation at Robot Innovation Week 2024

Specially-Appointed Associate Professor Hashimoto gave a presentation on laboratory automation using robotic arms, invited by TechShare Corporation.

The presentation introduced examples of automated experimental systems developed in the laboratory and low-cost automation methods.

August 4, 2024

Establishment Commemorative Symposium Held

We held a commemorative symposium for the establishment of the endowed research division within the Frontier Research Institute for Interdisciplinary Sciences.

The symposium presented the division's founding purpose and future research directions, fostering discussions on creating the new interdisciplinary field of materials process data science.
